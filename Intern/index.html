Intern FAQ

Q: Should I pitch individually or in a group?
A: We strongly encourage you to pitch in subteams (2+ people). Group pitches usually result in stronger ideas and better collaboration over the summer.

Q: What’s the guidance on forming groups for pitching?
A: After pitch presentations, the mentor team will review ideas. If multiple subteams propose similar projects, we may suggest: Collaborating to form a larger group, exploring different angles of the same topic, and/or pivoting to other projects if one is already well-covered.

Q: When is my pitch presentation due?
A: Plan to have your pitch presentation ready by the 2nd or 3rd meeting. The first meeting is meant for discussing potential projects. Use Slack #project-scoping and office hours for help.

Q: When is the design document due?
A: A draft design document should be ready by your pitch presentation. Finalize it based on mentor feedback by the end of Week 3.

Q: Can I work on more than one project this summer?
A: We recommend focusing on one project for the summer. This allows for deeper engagement and more meaningful outcomes.

Q: Can I continue my work after the end of the summer?
A: Yes! We often invite students whose projects are nearing publication to continue their work with us into the fall semester or beyond to complete their manuscript and submit for publication.

Q: Will I receive a letter of recommendation?
A: Yes! — if you meet all expectations (see below).

Letter of Recommendation FAQ

Q: Will interns receive a letter of recommendation after the program?
A: Yes! We are committed to writing stellar letters of recommendation to support your college applications and future opportunities. Interns who fully participate in the program and meet all expectations will be eligible.

Q: What are the expectations to earn a letter of recommendation?
To be eligible, interns must meet the following program expectations:

  • Weekly Check-Ins: Stay in regular contact with your research mentor through weekly check-ins.
  • Seminar Attendance: Attend all scheduled seminars and actively engage by asking questions to speakers.
  • Team Engagement: Introduce yourself to your project group during the first week and contribute meaningfully throughout. Regular attendance at team meetings is a must. Be an effective member of the team (interpersonal skills, play nice!).
  • Project Participation: Spend the majority of your time working on your research project(s) and mini-projects with your team.
  • End-of-Summer Symposium: Attend and participate in the final symposium event.
  • Deliverables: Complete all assigned writing and presentation tasks (see below).

Q: What are the key deliverables for the program?
All interns are expected to complete the following:

Early Program (All Tracks)
  • Draft a design document for your project.
  • Create and present a pitch presentation.
  • Introduce yourself to your project group during the first week.
  • Engage in seminars by attending and asking thoughtful questions.
Writing Deliverables (by track):
  • All Tracks: Research abstract
  • Skills Track: Blog post
  • Advanced Track: Research manuscript
Final Presentations (all tracks)
  • Create a poster summarizing your work.
  • Record a 10–15 minute PowerPoint video presentation explaining your project, methods, and outcomes.
  • Present your work at the End-of-Summer Symposium.

What happens if I meet these expectations?

If you meet the expectations and complete your deliverables, you’ll be eligible for a strong letter of recommendation from our team. We love to recognize and support hard-working interns who contribute meaningfully to their research teams!

What if I fall short on some expectations?

While we understand challenges may arise, active participation, communication, and effort throughout the summer are key to receiving a recommendation. Keep your mentors informed and stay engaged — we’re here to support you.
